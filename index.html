Eye

Anatomy of a human eye

The eye is a sensory organ that allows organisms to perceive visual information.
Part of the eye Description
Cornea It is the transparent front part of the eye. It allows light to enter and makes it focus on the retina
Retina Located at the back of the eyeball.It converts focused light into electrical impulses then sends it to the brain via optic nerves.
Lens the clear part of the eye behind the iris that helps to focus light, or an image, onto the retina.
Pupil The opening in the centre of the iris that lets in light. It is regulated by the iris.
Sclera It is the outer layer of the eye
Vitreous a clear gel that fills the inside of the eye and helps it to retain its shape.
Iris the coloured part of your eye that regulates the amount of light entering.

Color blindness

The human eye has 3 cones that pick up different color wavelengths. Red , Green and Blue . Each cone is sensitive to different wavelengths of light. When light enters the eye and reaches the retina, these cones respond by detecting how much of each color is present. The cones then send signals through the optic nerve to the brain, which combines the information to create the colors we see.

Color blindness occurs when 1 or more of the color cone cells are either absent , not working or detect a different color than it should.
The most common type of color blindness is red-green color blindness. Followed up blue-green. Complete color blindness, also known as achromatopsia, is rarer than the other two.
